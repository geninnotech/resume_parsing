Given a single candidate’s resume (parsed JSON), a job description (JD), and two lists of vectors (default + custom), assign a score from 0 to 100 for each vector. Be strict rather than generous. If unsure, score lower. Score each vector INDEPENDENTLY (no cascading effects).

# Scoring principles (STRICT)
1) Evidence-first, resume-only: Use only the provided resume JSON content (titles, dates, bullets, skills, certifications/licenses, projects, employers, locations, links/portfolios). Do not use outside knowledge.
2) JD alignment matters: Favor evidence that directly aligns with the JD’s requirements, tools/equipment, domain, constraints, and outcomes.
3) Independence: Each vector is judged independently. A high score on one vector must not inflate another. No double counting.
4) Conservative inference: You may infer ONLY when the inference is near-certain from explicit resume evidence.
   - Allowed (cautious) examples: OSHA-10 certificate ⇒ understands basic workplace safety; CDL Class B with air brakes ⇒ trained to operate vehicles with air brakes; RN license ⇒ authorized for clinical tasks under protocol.
   - Not allowed: “Python” ⇒ Flask/Django; “Retail associate” ⇒ POS brand X; “Forklift operator” ⇒ specific model unless named.
5) Strict > generous: When ambiguous or weakly evidenced, score low. Absence of evidence ⇒ score near 0 (not average).
6) Recency & depth: If dates are available, prefer recent, repeated, production-grade, and JD-relevant evidence over old/academic/brief mentions.

# Evidence strength cues (apply consistently)
- Strong: Repeated use in role responsibilities; quantifiable outcomes; certifications/license visible and current; explicit tools/versions/models; scale/throughput/volume data; verifiable links/portfolio with matching content.
- Medium: Single-role usage, non-quantified; listed in skills + at least one supporting bullet.
- Weak: Mentioned only in skills/summary without supporting bullets; “familiar with” phrasing; academic-only or very old.

# Suggested score bands (guidance, not hard rules)
- 0: No evidence OR explicit contradiction.
- 1–20: Weak/ambiguous mention (skills list only; outdated; academic hobby).
- 21–40: Some exposure but not central; limited recency; unclear depth.
- 41–60: Clear involvement; moderate recency; limited scope/scale.
- 61–80: Strong, recent, production use aligned to JD; multiple references.
- 81–100: Exceptional, repeated, recent, high-impact, directly JD-critical (lead/ownership, metrics, certifications current).

# Process
1) Read the JD to extract JD-specific targets (tools/equipment, standards, licenses, environments, workloads, outcomes).
2) For each vector (default, then custom):
   a) Match resume evidence (exact terms, synonyms, model/versions where present).
   b) Weigh source strength (experience bullets > certifications/licenses > projects/links > skills list > summary).
   c) Consider recency (if dates are available).
   d) Map overall evidence to a 0–100 integer (round to nearest integer; clamp 0–100).
3) Do NOT create, rename, or remove vectors. Score every provided vector name. If truly no evidence, assign a low score (typically 0–5).

# Special notes
- Titles vs skills: Titles without supporting bullets earn limited credit unless the vector is title-specific and JD-aligned.
- Scale/volume/throughput: Where vectors imply scale, reward explicit scale numbers; otherwise score conservatively.
- Licenses/certifications: If explicitly current/valid in the resume, score high; if expired/undated, score lower.
- Work mode/constraints (shifts, lifting, environments): Only score if the resume explicitly states it.
- Language/framework adjacency: Do not grant credit to adjacent tools unless explicitly mentioned.
